## Design Context

### Users

Watum is an academic operations product used primarily by lecturers and administrators, with students also accessing the system to review their schedules. The most important jobs center on checking class utilization, understanding schedule availability, and making better classroom allocation decisions without unnecessary friction.

### Brand Personality

The interface should feel academic, sleek, and calming. It should communicate institutional credibility and operational clarity without becoming cold, overloaded, or visually theatrical.

### Aesthetic Direction

This product should establish the first real design language for the whole application. The visual direction should feel like a modern academic system rather than a generic startup dashboard: structured, composed, readable, and trustworthy, with full support for mobile layouts, light and dark themes, and dynamic text lengths. The primary interface language is Indonesian.

### Design Principles

1. Lead with clarity over decoration: scheduling and utilization information should be immediately legible.
2. Make operational decisions feel calm: dense data is acceptable, but the interface should reduce stress through hierarchy and restraint.
3. Build one system for multiple roles: lecturers, admins, and students should recognize the same product language even when task depth differs.
4. Design for real academic complexity: variable schedules, room states, edge cases, and longer labels must fit naturally.
5. Establish reusable foundations first: this feature should define patterns that can scale across the rest of the product.
